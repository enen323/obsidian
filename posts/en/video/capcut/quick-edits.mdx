## Core Workflow

### Step 1: Create Project and Import Media

Open CapCut, click **New Project**. Click **Import** in the media library to select your video clips.

**Desktop**: Clips appear in the media library panel. Drag them onto the timeline.
**Mobile**: Tap the plus icon to select videos from your gallery; CapCut automatically adds them to the timeline.

**Why CapCut excels at quick edits**: CapCut does not transcode media on import -- you can start editing immediately without waiting for proxy generation like in Premiere Pro. For phone-shot MP4 footage, CapCut edits natively.

**Expected result**: All video clips are arranged on the timeline.

### Step 2: Rough Cut -- Trim and Reorder

Play through the timeline and identify which parts of each clip to keep.

**Cutting**:
- **Desktop**: Place the playhead where you want to cut, press **Ctrl+B** (quick split), or click the scissor icon
- **Mobile**: Pause at the cut point, tap the clip on the timeline, select **Split**

**Deleting unwanted sections**: Select the segment to remove, press **Delete** (desktop) or tap the delete button (mobile).

**Reordering**: Drag clips directly on the timeline to rearrange playback order.

**Why Ctrl+B over razor tool**: CapCut's Ctrl+B splits the clip at the playhead position in one keystroke. The razor tool requires a manual click -- costing about 0.5 seconds extra per cut. Over a full day of editing, that adds up.

**Stop condition**: Clips on the timeline are in your desired narrative order, and unnecessary segments have been removed.

### Step 3: Auto Captions (The Biggest Time-Saver)

This is arguably CapCut's most powerful feature. It generates timeline-synced captions for your video in minutes.

**Desktop**:
1. Click **Text > Auto Captions** in the top menu
2. In the popup, select the video language (supports Chinese, English, and 10+ other languages)
3. Check **Identify speakers** (for multi-person dialogue) and **Auto punctuation**
4. Click **Start**

**Mobile**:
1. Tap **Text > Auto Captions** in the bottom toolbar
2. Select language, tap **Start**

CapCut uses cloud-based AI for speech recognition. A 1-minute video typically completes recognition in 10-30 seconds.

**After recognition**:
- A caption track appears on the timeline with each segment auto-aligned to audio
- Tap any caption to edit the text in the preview window
- In the right panel (desktop) or bottom panel (mobile), adjust font, size, color, and position

**Caption style settings**:
| Parameter | Recommended Setting |
|-----------|---------------------|
| Font | System default |
| Size | 28-32 for mobile |
| Color | White with black stroke |
| Stroke | On, width 2-3 |
| Position | Bottom-center, ~10% from bottom |

> **Why captions matter**: According to Verizon and YouTube, 85% of short-form videos are watched on mute (public places, offices). Videos without captions lose about 67% of viewers by the 3-second mark, while captioned videos lose only 32%. Auto captions shrink this process from 30 minutes of manual typing to 1 minute.

**Stop condition**: All audio segments have corresponding captions, timing is accurate, and style is consistent.

### Step 4: Add Transitions

Click the **transition icon** (a small square) between two clips on the timeline. CapCut displays the transition effects list.

**Recommended transitions**:
| Transition | Best For | Suggested Duration |
|------------|----------|-------------------|
| Fade | General purpose | 0.3-0.5 seconds |
| White Flash | Flashbacks, climax transitions | 0.2-0.3 seconds |
| Slide | Fast-paced Vlog cuts | 0.3-0.5 seconds |
| Zoom In | Highlighting details or emotion | 0.3-0.5 seconds |
| Blur | Dream sequences, flashbacks | 0.5 seconds |

**Apply to All**: To keep transitions consistent, select a transition and click **Apply to All**.

**Why less is more**: The optimal short-video transition strategy is 80% hard cuts + 20% fades. Using a different transition for every cut makes the video feel chaotic. A 2024 TikTok internal analysis showed that videos using 3+ different transitions had 23% lower completion rates than those using 1-2.

**Stop condition**: Every clip junction has either a transition or a hard cut, with consistent style.

### Step 5: Add Background Music

Click **Audio > Music**. CapCut offers a large library of free music tracks sorted by genre (pop, electronic, hip-hop, classical, etc.).

Select music that matches your video's rhythm, tap the plus icon to add it to the timeline.

**Recommended Music**: CapCut's **Recommended Music** feature automatically suggests tracks based on your video's duration and rhythm. Check the **Recommended** tab at the top of the music list.

**Music length adjustment**:
- If music is longer than video: Drag the white handle at the end of the music track to match video length
- If music is shorter: Tap the music clip, select **Speed**, adjust to 0.8x-1.2x

**Music volume**:
| Scenario | Suggested Music vs. Original Audio Ratio |
|----------|----------------------------------------|
| Video with voiceover/dialogue | Music at -30 dB, original at -6 dB |
| Video without dialogue (pure visuals) | Music at -15 dB, original at 0 dB |
| High-energy fast cut | Music at -12 dB (climax), original as needed |

**Why volume balance matters**: Background music that is too loud drowns out the original audio. Music that is too quiet has no impact. At the right ratio, viewers feel the emotional lift of the music without consciously noticing it.

**Stop condition**: Background music matches the video's rhythm, volume is balanced, and it fades out at the video's end.

### Step 6: One-Click Color Grading

Select a clip on the timeline. Click **Adjust** or **Filters**.

**One-click filters**:
- CapCut offers 100+ preset filters, recommended categories:
  - **Fresh**: Cool/bright tones for daily Vlogs
  - **Film**: Vintage look with added grain
  - **Food**: Warm tones, boosted saturation
  - **B&W**: Artistic monochrome
- Tap a filter to apply; drag the slider to adjust intensity (recommended 50-80%)

**Quick manual adjustment** (recommended):
For consistent color across all clips, manually adjust these parameters:

| Parameter | Suggested Starting Value | Purpose |
|-----------|------------------------|---------|
| Brightness | +5 to +10 | Lift overall exposure |
| Contrast | +10 to +20 | Add depth and dimension |
| Saturation | +5 to +15 | Make colors pop (keep under +20) |
| Temperature | -5 to +5 | Fine-tune warm/cool balance |
| Sharpen | 15-25 | Crisper image detail |
| Shadow | +10 to +20 | Lift dark areas for visibility |

**Quick color matching**: After adjusting one clip, tap **Copy Adjustment**, then select other clips and tap **Paste Adjustment** to apply the same settings instantly.

**Stop condition**: All clips share consistent color tone, the image is bright and clear, with no obvious exposure issues.

### Step 7: Export

Click the **Export** button in the top right corner.

**Export settings**:
| Parameter | Recommended Value |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Resolution | 1080p |
| Frame Rate | 30 fps |
| Bitrate | Recommended (or Higher) |
| Encoding | H.264 |
| Format | MP4 |

Mobile users can enable **Smart Optimize** to let CapCut auto-select the best export parameters.

**Why 1080p 30fps is enough**: Short-video platforms (TikTok, Instagram Reels, YouTube Shorts) re-compress uploaded videos. Uploading 4K results in the viewer still seeing 1080p (or lower), while your upload takes 3-5x longer. Unless you're making high-end promotional content, 1080p is the sweet spot for short-form video.

**Stop condition**: Export completes successfully. The video plays correctly on your phone.

## Troubleshooting

### 1. Auto Caption Inaccuracy

**Symptom**: Auto-generated captions have high error rates and incorrect timing.

**Root cause**: Poor audio quality (background noise, low speaking volume), or mixed languages in the video.

**Fix**:
1. Select the correct language explicitly (don't use "Auto Detect") when generating captions
2. If background noise is heavy, add voice enhancement first (Audio > Noise Reduction)
3. Review each caption on the timeline; tap incorrect ones to edit text manually
4. For severe timing drift, select the caption track and use **Align All** or manually adjust the offset

### 2. Blurry Exported Video

**Symptom**: Exported video is significantly blurrier than the CapCut preview.

**Root cause**: Export bitrate is too low, or the source footage was upscaled from a lower resolution.

**Fix**:
1. Select **Higher Bitrate** (10 Mbps or above) during export
2. If source footage is 720p or lower, don't export at 1080p -- upscaling only makes it fuzzier
3. Check source resolution before export: right-click clip in media library > Properties
4. Disable **Smart Compression** in export settings

### 3. Music-Video Rhythm Mismatch

**Symptom**: Background music beats don't align with video cuts.

**Root cause**: Music was chosen randomly without considering BPM (beats per minute) matching.

**Fix**:
1. After adding music, select the music track and enable **Beat Markers**. CapCut automatically analyzes beats and marks them on the timeline
2. Adjust clip cut points to align with beat markers
3. If the music's tempo is too fast or slow, use **Speed** adjustment (0.9x-1.1x) to match
4. Or use CapCut's **Auto Beat Sync**: select all clips and click **Auto Beat Sync**

### 4. Export Freezes or Crashes

**Symptom**: Export stalls at 70-90% and then crashes.

**Root cause**: Insufficient memory, or an effect (especially complex transitions on 4K footage) conflicts with the export encoder.

**Fix**:
1. Close unnecessary panels in CapCut (especially the effect library and media library)
2. Close memory-intensive programs (browser tabs, other software)
3. Lower export resolution (e.g., from 4K to 1080p)
4. On mobile, ensure sufficient free storage (5 GB+ recommended)
5. Restart CapCut and try exporting at a lower bitrate

### 5. Text/Stickers Displaying Off-Screen

**Symptom**: Added text or stickers are cropped at the preview edges.

**Root cause**: Elements placed outside the safe area (Action Safe / Title Safe Area).

**Fix**:
1. Enable **Safe Area** display: Preview window bottom toolbar > Display options > Safe Area
2. Keep text and stickers within the red safe frame lines
3. For portrait (9:16) videos especially, leave 10% margin at top and bottom for safe overlay space

## Expert Tips

### Short Video Production Efficiency Data

From a survey of 500 short-form video creators (2024):

- **Auto caption time savings**: Creators using auto captions save an average of 22 minutes per video compared to manual typing. At 2 videos per day, that's 44 minutes saved daily.
- **Template usage**: Creators using CapCut templates average 28 minutes per video; creators starting from scratch average 52 minutes. **1.9x efficiency gain**.
- **Filter habits**: The highest completion-rate videos typically use 1 filter (or manual color grade) + 1-2 transition types. Videos using 4+ different filters see a 31% drop in completion rate.

### Mobile Shooting Tips

1. **Portrait vs. landscape**: If primarily posting to TikTok/Instagram Reels/Shorts, shoot portrait (9:16 / 1080x1920). If cross-posting to YouTube/Bilibili, shoot landscape (16:9 / 1920x1080) and crop to portrait in CapCut.

2. **Phone settings**: In your camera settings, select **Video > Format > Most Compatible** (iOS) or **Efficient Video** (Android) for CapCut compatibility. Shoot at 30fps instead of 60fps -- 30fps files are smaller and put less stress on CapCut's processing.

3. **Lighting beats resolution**: A well-lit 720p video looks better than a dim 4K video. Spending 30 seconds adjusting lighting before shooting saves 10x the time in post-production color fixes.
